Groups:

We offer support groups for children ages three through nineteen. We have separate groups for children and teens that are grieving the loss of someone they love because of death, divorce or separation. We also help children who are in foster care, and those with anticipitory grief.

A typical group includes up to 10 children, 5 trained volunteer facilitators and staff. An adult family member or care-giver must accompany each child and may participate in the adult support group which meets simultaneously. For Grieving Children.

Group Schedules:

Grief Support Groups:

For children, teens, and their parents or guardians grieving due to the death of a loved one.

A Center For Grieving Children offers mutual support and acceptance through listening and sharing. We believe grief is a natural reaction to the loss of a loved one for children as well as adults and that acceptance and caring assist in the healing process. Our goals are to support and nurture children and their families in a loving and understanding environment as they experience grief.

Groups and Times:

  • Youth A (ages 3-12) meet the 1st and 3rd Wednesday of each month from 6:00 to 7:30 pm.
  • Youth B (ages 3-12) Meet the 2nd and 4th Thursday of each month from 6:00 to 7:30 pm.
  • Teens (ages 13-19) meet the 1st and 3rd Wednesday of each month from 6:00 to 7:30 pm.
  • Adults meet at the same time as the children or teen groups.
